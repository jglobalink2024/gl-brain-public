## Opening scripts

**Standard (non-military referral):**
"Thanks for making time. I'm in research mode — trying to understand how consulting firms like yours are actually managing their AI stacks as they get more complex. No agenda, no demo. I'm going to ask you a few questions and listen. Is it okay if I take notes? [pause] Great. And just to set expectations — this is genuinely just a conversation. I may share what I'm building at the end if you're curious, but that's not why we're here."

**Military referral (Grant Carlson and similar):**
"Grant — Blaz connected us, appreciate you taking the time. I'll keep it simple: I'm in research mode right now, trying to understand how operators like you are actually managing their AI stacks day to day. No pitch, no demo. I just want to hear how it's working for you. Is now still good?"

**NOTE:** Do not lean on shared military background beyond the opener. Acknowledge briefly, then move to business. Hollow "hooah" framing creates distance with civilian operators.

## Five core discovery questions

**Q1 — Stack Inventory (3 min)**
"Walk me through the AI tools you're actually running right now — not what you're evaluating, what's live and being used in a typical week."

Listen for:
- GREEN: 3+ tools named (Claude, Perplexity, GPT-4, Cursor, Clay, Apollo, Gong)
- GREEN: "I have Claude open in three tabs" — multi-instance signal
- GREEN: Any mention of having deleted or simplified their stack
- YELLOW: 1–2 tools — ask single-instance follow-up: "Are you running multiple instances of that at the same time — like, do you have it open in more than one tab doing different things?"
- RED: No AI tools live yet — downgrade to warm follow-up

**Q2 — The Relay Question (5 min)**
"When work moves between those tools — or between sessions — what does that actually look like? Walk me through what you do."

Listen for:
- GREEN: "I copy and paste it over" / "I re-explain everything" / "I have to brief it again from scratch"
- GREEN: Any manual information transfer description
- GREEN: "I swear to god if I have to do this one more time" energy
- Probe if GREEN: "How long does that relay step take relative to the actual work?" / "What would break if you didn't do that step?"

**Q3 — The Failure Story (5 min)**
"Has there been a time where something dropped in that handoff — where context got lost or a task fell through the gap between tools?"

Listen for:
- GREEN: Specific story — deliverable rebuilt, session started over, dollar/hour estimate of cost
- YELLOW: "Not really" → follow up: "What do you do to make sure that doesn't happen?" (Prevention ritual often reveals pain more than the failure does.)

**Q4 — The Visibility Question (3 min)**
"Right now, if I asked you what your AI agents are doing at this exact moment — could you tell me?"

Listen for:
- GREEN: "No idea" / "I'd have to open all the tabs" / "I just hope it's running"
- GREEN: Laughter or self-deprecation — confirms pain viscerally
- GREEN: "I've started keeping a spreadsheet just to track it"

**Q5 — The Vision Question (2 min)**
"If you had a single place where you could see what every agent was doing, route work to the right one, and have context carry automatically between them — what would be different about your week?"

Listen for:
- GREEN: Hour or dollar estimate — "I spend 5–10 hours a week just on this"
- GREEN: "I'd sleep better" — emotional validation signal
- RED: Blank or confused — may not have the pain at scale yet

## Deflect-back protocol (if they ask what COMMAND does mid-call)

"I'll tell you exactly what I'm building at the end — but I want to make sure I understand your situation first, or what I tell you won't be relevant. Can I ask you one more thing?"

## Non-pitch close (18-min mark)

**Standard close:**
"This has been genuinely useful — you've confirmed several things I was only guessing at. I'm building something that addresses exactly what you described. It works with whatever tools you're already running — Claude, ChatGPT, or any combination. That's intentional. It's early and I'm selective about who I bring in. Would it be okay if I sent you a one-pager and followed up in a week or two to see if it's relevant to where you are?"

**Pilot framing close (when pain confirmed + interest high):**
"I'm running a limited pilot right now — 25 operators, locked pricing, and I'm genuinely trying to learn from people who have the problem. Based on what you just described, I think you'd get real value from it. Want me to send you the details?"

**IMPORTANT:** Say "pilot" not "trial." Consultants use "pilot" with their own clients — signals peer sophistication, lowers identity exposure.

**Model-agnostic inoculation (always say this):**
"Whatever tools you're already running — Claude, ChatGPT, or any combination. That's intentional. COMMAND is the coordination layer above all of them." (Prevents "I'll just use Claude Projects" objection before it surfaces.)

## Objection handling

**"Won't Anthropic / OpenAI just build this?"**
"They literally can't solve the cross-vendor problem — a single vendor can only coordinate its own tools. That's a feature, not a coordination layer. COMMAND sits above all of them."

**"What about Claude Managed Agents?"**
"Anthropic just shipped that on April 8 — and it actually validates exactly what I'm building. CMA is a developer API for running Claude agents on Anthropic's infrastructure. Claude-only, $0.08/hr. Great for engineering teams. But if you're running Claude AND GPT AND Perplexity AND Cursor — which is what most operators are doing — CMA doesn't help with the coordination. COMMAND is the layer that connects all of them."
(This objection is a GIFT. It means they're paying attention to the space. Lean in.)

**"I'll just use Claude Projects."**
"Claude Projects coordinates Claude instances. COMMAND coordinates Claude, GPT, Perplexity, Cursor — whatever you're running. Model-agnostic by design."

**"I don't have time to set up another tool."**
"The pilot takes 20 minutes to connect your first agent. That's it. Most operators tell us they get that time back in the first week."

**"I've been burned by tools before."**
"That's exactly why I'm running a limited pilot instead of an open launch. 25 operators, close feedback loop, I'm personally accountable for your result. It's not a product launch — it's a research partnership."

## Post-call scoring (ICP score guide)

Score 8–10: Invite to pilot immediately.
Score 5–7: Send one-pager. Follow up in 7 days.
Score 1–4: Park. Warm follow-up in 30 days or drop.

Post-call synthesis template fields: Firm name, sub-vertical, firm size, AI tools live, multi-instance confirmed, double-pain operator, deletion signal, relay method, failure story, hours/week estimate, vision response, ICP score, agency signal, beta/pilot interest, referral offered, follow-up action + date.

## Fratricide pain story (founder-sourced, 260413)

**When to deploy:** After Q4 lands (Visibility Question). When prospect describes tool chaos. When prospect laughs about "hoping it's running" — escalate: "It gets worse. Let me tell you what happened to me last week."

**The Line (use verbatim):**
> "Two AI agents working on the same project committed each other's work without knowing the other existed. I spent the next hour untangling it. That's the moment I knew — if I'm the only thing preventing my agents from stepping on each other, I'm not the boss. I'm the traffic cop."

**What it validates:** RELAY FAILURE + VISIBILITY FAILURE. Product thesis: COMMAND is air traffic control. When you route through it, it's the conductor. When you don't, you get fratricide.
